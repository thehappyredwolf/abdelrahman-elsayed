The first time I heard about Twenty One Pilots was when I was in high school. A friend of mine, who was older than me, told me to listen to a song called The Judge. At that time, I wasn’t really used to listening to English music at all. Most of the music I used to listen to was in Arabic.

But when I heard that song, something felt different. I didn’t just like it… I ended up listening to the whole album in the same sitting. That was the first time I really started getting into an English album.

After that, I started exploring their older albums too, one by one. From that moment, I became a big fan of them.

The main thing that caught my attention was their sound. The way the two members, Tyler and Josh, work together felt very unique to me. The voice changes, the energy going up and down, and how the music is not just a simple beat. It feels alive. There are many small details in their songs that make it different from anything I heard before.

I also liked that they are not just about music. Their whole style is special. The way they choose album names, the visuals, their clothing, and even their live shows. Everything feels carefully designed but also a bit strange in a good way. For example, they do things like performing in unusual places or using creative ideas in their performances. It always feels different and fresh.

One song that really represents a period of my life is Stressed Out. This song described a time when I was feeling very stressed, anxious, and kind of stuck in my thoughts. Back then, their music helped me calm down and think in a different way. It was one of the reasons I started to love music more seriously, not just as background sound, but as something that can actually affect how you feel.

Over time, their music became more than just songs for me. It became a place where I can relax and reset my mind. They also made me realize that music can be real art, where every detail matters, not just the melody.

Now, one of my biggest dreams is to attend one of their live concerts. I watch their performances on YouTube sometimes, and I always feel like it must be an amazing experience to see them live. Right now I am in Egypt, and I still have a long way ahead of me, but this is one of my real goals for the future.
